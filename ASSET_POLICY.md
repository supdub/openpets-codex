# Asset and IP policy

The root MIT license covers this repository's software and documentation. Artwork, spritesheets,
previews, character designs, names, and other media under `pets/` use the terms declared in each
pet's `LICENSE-ASSETS.md` and `pet.meta.json`.

## Preferred submissions

- Original characters released as CC0, CC BY 4.0, or another redistribution-friendly license.
- Commissioned work whose contract explicitly allows redistribution.
- AI-assisted work with honest tool/provenance disclosure and no unlicensed input images.

## Third-party characters and brands

Fan art does not make the underlying character, costume, logo, or franchise open-source. Such a
pet must be clearly unofficial, non-endorsed, separately licensed/noticed, and removable on a
good-faith rights-holder request. Acceptance is not a legal opinion and does not guarantee that
every jurisdiction recognizes the same exceptions.

Do not submit extracted game assets, official animation frames, traced art, copyrighted
screenshots, or trademark-heavy designs without written permission. Maintainers may reject or
remove a pet when provenance or rights are uncertain.

## Living people

A recognizable living-person likeness is not an original character and needs its own disclosure.
Use a custom asset notice that identifies the subject, states whether the work is caricature or
portraiture, and avoids implying endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation. Do not use private or
unlicensed photographs, logos, signature brand assets, or misleading captions as inputs.

Contributors may license only the original rendering rights they hold. A pet notice cannot grant
the subject's name, likeness, privacy, publicity, trademark, or false-endorsement rights, which
vary by jurisdiction. Prefer respectful, non-defamatory depictions, mark them unofficial, and
provide the same good-faith takedown path used for fan content.

## Required metadata

Every pet declares:

- author(s) and version;
- asset license or custom notice;
- the SHA-256 digest of the exact spritesheet;
- whether AI assisted the work;
- whether a third-party character is depicted;
- confirmation that the contributor may submit and redistribute the files;
- the Codex desktop version used for the final smoke test.
